Green Hydrogen Fueling a Sustainable Future

A sustainable system for producing green hydrogen, mainly powered by solar energy, designed and implemented by Suez Canal University students.

Explore Our Journey Meet Our Team

The Green Hydrogen Journey

Follow our interactive flowchart to discover how we transform water into clean, renewable hydrogen energy through innovative solar-powered technology.

Step 1

From Saline Water to Pure H₂O

Our project begins with a crucial step: desalinating water, which then feeds into the electrolyzer. This clean water is essential for efficient hydrogen production.

Water Quality Data:

Sea Water (before distillation): pH 8.3, TDS 42,000 mg/L, Conductivity 55,000 µS/cm
Distilled Water (after distillation): pH 6.5, TDS 25 mg/L, Conductivity 10 µS/cm
Step 2

Efficient Water Purification with Solar Still

Our design combines a solar still for desalinating water, uniquely enhanced by using waste heat from a nearby power station (around 60°C) to boost the process and produce more fresh water.

Results:

Normal Distillation Efficiency: Approximately 21.8%
Distillation with Heater Efficiency: Approximately 55.6%
Step 3

Harnessing the Sun: Our PV System

Electricity for our electrolysis process is generated by solar panels, ensuring a renewable and clean energy source.

We used 72-cell solar modules with 36 volts
Step 4

Splitting Water, Creating Green H₂

The clean water feeds into the electrolyser, where electricity from the solar panels splits it into hydrogen and oxygen. We chose the alkaline electrolyser because it is cost-effective, easy to build and maintain, offers high hydrogen output relative to its size, and works efficiently with aqueous solutions.

Innovation & Results:

Innovative Electrode Material: We introduced a new electrode material—a nickel-coated stainless-steel alloy
Significant Efficiency Improvement: This significantly improved the system's efficiency, raising it from 79% to 82%
Hydrogen Production Rate: Our system can produce around 250 liters of hydrogen per hour
Step 5

Our Contribution: Clean Energy for Tomorrow

The produced green hydrogen is a powerful, nearly clean energy source, with zero carbon emissions during production and use when powered by renewables.

Key Benefits:

Flexible Energy Storage
High Energy Content
Decarbonizing Tough Industries
Boosting Energy Independence
Fighting Climate Change
